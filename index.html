About Me

Know more about me

I am a geospatial data scientist with a strong background in hydrology and modeling. My expertise lies in applying advanced geospatial techniques and machine learning algorithms to solve complex environmental challenges, particularly in water resources and natural hazards. I am passionate about leveraging data and technology to create innovative solutions for a sustainable future.

Currently, I am working as a Postdoctoral Researcher at INRAE, France, focusing on flood forecasting using deep learning models. Prior to this, I was a MOPGA-GRI Fellow at CNRM, Météo-France, where I developed early warning flood risk maps. I hold a Ph.D. in Geography from Banaras Hindu University, India, specializing in flood hazard and land change modeling.

With several years of research experience, I have published numerous SCI journal articles and book chapters. I have presented my work at international conferences and workshops, and have been recognized with several awards and honors, including the Marie-Curie Co-fund SMASH Postdoc Award and the Make Our Planet Green Again Fellowship. Beyond research, I have experience in administrative roles, training, and freelance GIS consulting. I am also a FIDE-rated chess player, bringing strategic thinking and analytical skills to all my endeavors.
